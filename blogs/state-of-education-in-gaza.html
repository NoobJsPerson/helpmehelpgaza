Education in Gaza: Challenges and Resilience

Introduction

The Gaza Strip, a densely populated coastal enclave, faces significant challenges in its education system. Ongoing conflicts, economic hardships, and political instability have deeply impacted the lives of students and educators. In this article, we delve into the state of education in Gaza, highlighting both the struggles and the resilience of its educational community.

UNRWA's Role in Education

The United Nations Relief and Works Agency for Palestine Refugees in the Near East (UNRWA) plays a crucial role in providing education to Palestinian children in Gaza. As of the 2021/2022 school year, UNRWA serves 291,100 students (150,608 males and 140,492 females) across 183 schools in the Gaza Strip. These schools operate under challenging conditions, with class sizes averaging 41.2 students per class¹.

Challenges Faced by Students

  1. Psychological Trauma: Recurrent hostilities, including conflicts in 2008/09, 2012/14, and 2021, have left lasting scars on Gaza's children. The May 2021 incident resulted in the loss of lives and injuries, affecting the emotional well-being of students. A survey found that 42% of children needed psychosocial intervention, even nine months later, with 35% still experiencing trauma reactions¹.
  2. Economic Hardships: The dire economic situation exacerbates the risk of student dropouts. Some families struggle to afford school materials or transportation costs. Boys often feel pressured to contribute to family income, impacting their ability to attend school regularly.
  3. Blockade and Restricted Movement: Gaza's ongoing blockade, now in its fifteenth year, severely restricts freedom of movement, cripples the economy, and leaves many dependent on humanitarian aid. Students face challenges in accessing educational resources and opportunities.

Impact on School Infrastructure

  1. Emergency Shelters: During conflicts, 90% of school buildings in Gaza serve as emergency shelters for internally displaced persons. This dual use disrupts regular schooling, affecting 400,700 students².
  2. School Damage: Since October 7, 342 schools have been damaged, with eight completely destroyed². The destruction of educational infrastructure hampers learning and stability.

Education Reform and Resilience

  1. Teacher Development: UNRWA has implemented the Teacher Policy, focusing on professional development. School-based teacher programs allow educators to experiment with new approaches in the classroom.
  2. Child-Centered Approach: Despite challenges, schools remain places where students feel safe and secure. Mental health and psychosocial support services are essential for addressing trauma and promoting well-being.
  3. Advocacy for Children's Rights: UNRWA advocates for the rights of children, emphasizing protection from violence and the importance of education.

Conclusion

Education in Gaza faces immense hurdles, but the resilience of students, teachers, and the UNRWA community shines through. As we continue to address these challenges, let us recognize the power of education to transform lives and build hope for a better future.
